R & MM Research Group,Vrije Universiteit Brussel, Belgium

3D printing of Self Healing Soft Robotics

Soft robots, unlike the traditional industrial robots are compliant and less harmful for human interaction. Use of Self-healing polymers in these robots will have an added advantage of being reparable. The Diels-Alder reaction is the basis of the self-healing technique. 3D printing of these polymers into soft robots is focused in this work. Different printing parameters are probed into. Lastly, these robots will be equipped with sensors for pressure and damage detection.

May 2019 - Aug 2019
Musculoskeletal Biomechanics Research Lab, McGill University, Canada

Robotic Spinal Cord

Spinal cord is one of the major components of the human body which plays a role in the human stability and dexterity. Because a cadaver cannot be used for research activities every time, a benchtop model of the spine consisting of ribcage, erector spinae and an abdomen with artificial muscles (PAMs) is built which would be helpful in evaluating parameters like intradiscal, Intramuscular pressures, the deflection of the spine under different loading scenarios. A control algorithm is implemented on the benchtop model and integration of position and force sensors, microfluidic valves for pressure control in the muscles with a PID controller in LabVIEW is done
